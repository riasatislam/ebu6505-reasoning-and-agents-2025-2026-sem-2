- Timing target (min): 71

## Exercises

### Q1 Starter Scenario

**Prompt**

Imagine a robot that must make tea in your room. In the light of today's lecture, what would it need to know before acting, what would it need to check while acting, and what could still go wrong if the knowledge were badly represented?

**Task details**

- Type: concept
- Mode: pair
- Time: 10 min
- Deliverable: short scenario analysis
- Assessment focus: explain what knowledge a robot needs before it can act reliably
- Expected length: 3 knowledge items + 2 checks + 1 risk

**How to submit**

Post one pair Padlet comment with three knowledge items, two runtime checks, and one likely failure.

**Discussion in class**

Which missing fact would break the task first?

**Why this helps**

Useful for short questions that ask why knowledge representation matters before planning or action.

**References**

- Russell & Norvig (2021) AIMA 4e, Ch.10, pp.314-343
- Poole & Mackworth (2023) 3e, Ch.16, pp.701-730

### Q2 Structured Problem

**Prompt**

Describe how to make a cup of tea as a series of short facts rather than commands. Write eight facts that a robot could store and later reason over.

**Task details**

- Type: problem
- Mode: individual
- Time: 10 min
- Deliverable: fact list
- Assessment focus: convert an action description into stored knowledge facts
- Expected length: 8 short facts

**How to submit**

Post your own Padlet comment as eight short facts, not instructions.

**Discussion in class**

Which of your facts describes a relation between two entities?

**Why this helps**

Direct practice for turning natural language into machine-usable facts.

**References**

- Russell & Norvig (2021) AIMA 4e, Ch.10, pp.314-343
- Poole & Mackworth (2023) 3e, Ch.16, pp.701-730

### Q3 Structured Problem

**Prompt**

A campus-support assistant must answer: Who teaches EBU6505? Where is the office of Dr_Riasat_Islam? When are EBU6505 office hours? Write 6-8 triples using clear IDs such as Module_EBU6505, Dr_Riasat_Islam, and TB_3_335.

**Task details**

- Type: problem
- Mode: individual
- Time: 12 min
- Deliverable: triple set with IDs
- Assessment focus: write clear triples using stable identifiers and meaningful relations
- Expected length: 6-8 triples + 1 ID note

**How to submit**

Post your own Padlet comment with the triples and one short note explaining the IDs you chose.

**Discussion in class**

Why are stable IDs better than storing only surface strings?

**Why this helps**

Direct practice for converting a simple support scenario into triples with explicit identifiers.

**References**

- Russell & Norvig (2021) AIMA 4e, Ch.10, pp.314-343
- Poole & Mackworth (2023) 3e, Ch.16, pp.701-730

### Q4 Stretch Extension

**Prompt**

A travel platform stores Hotel_H888, the city Beijing, a star rating, a user review, and a price band. Represent this as triples and make clear which parts are entity links and which parts are attributes.

**Task details**

- Type: stretch
- Mode: pair
- Time: 12 min
- Deliverable: mini data model
- Assessment focus: separate entities, attributes, and review facts in a realistic triple design
- Expected length: 6 triples + 2 attribute notes

**How to submit**

Post one pair Padlet comment with six triples and two short notes explaining attribute choices.

**Discussion in class**

Which information would you not model as a new entity?

**Why this helps**

Useful for questions about entity-versus-attribute choices and practical triple design.

**References**

- Russell & Norvig (2021) AIMA 4e, Ch.10, pp.314-343
- Poole & Mackworth (2023) 3e, Ch.16, pp.701-730

### Q5 Collaborative Mini-Case

**Prompt**

Design a small internal knowledge graph for a university assistant that answers timetable, office-hour, room-change, and contact questions. Include the node types, key relations, and a short retrieval flow.

**Task details**

- Type: case
- Mode: group
- Time: 15 min
- Deliverable: memory-graph design
- Assessment focus: design a small internal knowledge graph for an assistant with reusable memory
- Expected length: 4 node types + 5 relations + 3-step retrieval flow

**How to submit**

Post one group Padlet comment with four node types, five relations, and a three-step retrieval flow.

**Discussion in class**

What is the smallest graph that would still make the assistant useful?

**Why this helps**

Supports case questions on internal memory, graph structure, and retrieval design.

**References**

- Russell & Norvig (2021) AIMA 4e, Ch.10, pp.314-343
- Poole & Mackworth (2023) 3e, Ch.16, pp.701-730

### Q6 Exam-Style Constructed Response

**Prompt**

A university assistant can use timetable search, policy search, calculator, and email-draft tools. Explain how it should choose a tool and why planning should be separated from execution.

**Task details**

- Type: exam
- Mode: pair
- Time: 12 min
- Deliverable: structured explanation
- Assessment focus: explain tool-choice reasoning and why planning should be separated from execution
- Expected length: 180-240 words

**How to submit**

Post one pair Padlet comment with a clear policy, one failure case, and one recovery step.

**Discussion in class**

What goes wrong if the assistant executes a tool before it has a plan?

**Why this helps**

Matches longer answers about reasoning policies, tool selection, and controlled execution.

**References**

- Russell & Norvig (2021) AIMA 4e, Ch.10, pp.314-343
- Poole & Mackworth (2023) 3e, Ch.16, pp.701-730
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/intro-structured-data
